# Accountability Trigger Framework

## Purpose

This framework introduces practical examples of operational situations that may require governance escalation, human intervention or formal oversight review during AI system usage.

The objective is to support structured accountability approaches for organisations deploying AI-enabled systems and workflows.

---

# What is an Accountability Trigger?

An accountability trigger is an event, condition or observation that signals the need for additional human review, governance attention or operational escalation.

These triggers may help organisations identify situations where AI usage creates elevated operational, ethical, compliance or reputational concerns.

---

# Example Accountability Triggers

| Trigger Area | Example Trigger | Suggested Governance Response |
|---|---|---|
| Output Reliability | AI-generated information appears inaccurate or misleading | Require human validation before use |
| Human Oversight Failure | Users rely on AI outputs without review | Escalate for governance awareness |
| Sensitive Data Usage | AI system processes unexpected personal or confidential data | Conduct immediate governance review |
| Shadow AI Usage | Unapproved AI tools identified within teams | Assess governance and security risks |
| Decision Impact | AI-assisted output affects customer eligibility or access | Require secondary human approval |
| Repeated System Errors | Similar AI output failures occur repeatedly | Initiate operational review process |
| Regulatory Concern | AI usage creates uncertainty regarding compliance obligations | Escalate to governance or compliance lead |
| Vendor Dependency | Critical AI service disruption impacts operations | Activate contingency planning |

---

# Operational Review Considerations

Organisations may consider:

- defining escalation ownership
- documenting governance response actions
- maintaining incident records
- conducting periodic oversight reviews
- identifying high-risk AI activities
- clarifying human accountability responsibilities
